## Overview

My research is centered on **computational engineering design for energy systems**, combining classical optimization and heat transfer with modern machine-learning techniques. The goal is to make engineering design *faster, better, and more data-driven* — especially for thermal components and energy-intensive industrial processes.

---

## Research Areas

### 1. Topology Optimization for Thermal Systems

Developing new formulations and algorithms for the topology optimization of thermal components — including heat sinks, heat exchangers, and thermal insulators — under natural convection, forced convection, and multi-physics conditions. This line of work addresses both the mathematical formulation (objective functions, shape-dependent convection models) and the practical design of manufacturable geometries.

Representative papers: *IJHMT* (2015, 2016, 2017, 2018), *IEEE TCPMT* (2021), *EnOp* (2022), *Case Studies in Thermal Engineering* (2023), *Applied Thermal Engineering* (2025).

### 2. Machine Learning for Engineering Design

Building machine-learning methods that accelerate conventional numerical workflows — deep-learned finite elements, graph-based convergence acceleration for topology optimization, and deep reinforcement learning for design optimization. These methods reduce computational cost while maintaining the physical fidelity of engineering simulations.

Representative papers: *IEEE Access* (2021), *Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence* (2024), *International Communications in Heat and Mass Transfer* (2024), *Journal of Mechanical Science and Technology* (2025).

### 3. Industrial Energy Systems & Manufacturing

Data-driven modeling of energy-intensive manufacturing processes, including steel casting and drying, with the aim of improving energy efficiency and defining meaningful energy-based key performance indicators (KPIs) for industry.

Representative papers: *Journal of Cleaner Production* (2022), *Energy* (2024, 2025), *Journal of Dairy Science* (2024).

### 4. Heat Transfer & Thermal Management

Fundamental and applied research on heat transfer — natural convection around fins, pulsating heat pipes, phase-change materials, and thermoelectric generators. These studies provide the physical foundation used in the topology-optimization and machine-learning work above.

Representative papers: *IJHMT* (2018), *Energy Conversion and Management* (2018), *Energy* (2023).
