About

I am a Senior Researcher at the Korea Institute of Energy Research (KIER) and an Associate Professor at the University of Science & Technology (UST). My research sits at the intersection of computational engineering design, heat transfer, and machine learning.

I develop methods that make engineering design faster and better — especially topology optimization for thermal systems, deep-learning-accelerated finite element and optimization solvers, and data-driven energy modeling of industrial manufacturing processes such as casting and drying.

I received my B.S. (2012), M.S. (2014), and Ph.D. (2017) in Mechanical Engineering from KAIST. Before joining KIER in 2021, I was a Senior Researcher at the Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute (KAERI) and a postdoctoral researcher at KAIST.

Research Interests

  • Topology optimization — heat sinks, heat exchangers, thermal insulators, and multi-physics design.
  • Machine learning for engineering — deep-learned finite elements, graph-based convergence acceleration, reinforcement learning for design.
  • Industrial energy systems — energy-efficient steel casting, drying processes, and manufacturing KPIs.
  • Heat transfer & thermal management — natural convection, phase-change materials, solar thermoelectric generators.
